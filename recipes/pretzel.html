Pretzel

Description

A pretzel, pretzel or salty bow is a type of cookie or bun baked and twisted into a bow shape, with a slightly salty flavor. It originates from Germany and is quite popular in German-speaking Switzerland, Austria, Alsace and North America.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups water(360 mL), warm
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 4 ½ cups flour(565 g)
  • ⅔ cup baking soda(120 g)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3 tablespoons oil, divided

Steps

  1. In a largte bow, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let stand for 6 minutes, or until foamy
  2. Stir in the buttter, salt, and 2 cups of the flour. Stir until a dough forms.
  3. Trun the dough out onto a light floured surface and kned for 8-1 munutes
  4. Place the dough in a greased boel, cover with plastic wrap, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size
  5. Punch dow the dough and divide it into 12 equal pieces
    6. \
  6. Roll each piece of dough into a 12-inch rope.
  7. Form each rope into a pretzel shape, crossing the ends over each other and twisting them together.
  8. In a large pot, bring 2 quarts of water to a boil. Add the baking soda and stir until dissolved.
  9. Carefully drop the pretzels into the boiling water, one at a time. Let cook for 30 seconds per side, or until golden brown.
  10. Remove the pretzels from the boiling water with a slotted spoon and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  11. Brush the pretzels with the beaten egg and sprinkle with coarse salt.
  12. Bake for 15 -20 minutes, or until golden brown
  13. Let cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before serving.
