Sauerbraten

Description

Rheinischer Sauerbraten is a roast made from a meat marinade with vinegar, water, vegetable broth and spices left for several days. The resulting marinade tenderizes the meat little by little.

Ingredients

  • Beef: Start with a 3-pound beef rump roast.
  • Onions: Two chopped onions lend bold flavor.
  • Red wine vinegar: Red wine vinegar adds tang and helps tenderize the meat.
  • Water: Youâ€™ll need a cup of water for the marinade.
  • Seasonings: Season the marinade with salt, black pepper, whole cloves, and bay leaves.
  • Flour: All-purpose flour ensures perfectly browned meat.

Steps

  1. In a large pot, combine the red wine, red wine vinegar, onion, carrots, celery, bay leaf, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  2. Add the chuck roast to the pot and bring to a boil again. Reduce heat and simmer for 1 hour, or until the roast is browned on all sides.
  3. Remove the roast from the pot and let cool slightly.
  4. Pour the marinade into a large bowl and set aside.
  5. When the roast is cool enough to handle, trim any excess fat.
  6. Place the roast in a large baking dish and pour the marinade over it.
  7. Cover the baking dish with foil and refrigerate for at least 3 days, or up to 1 week
  8. Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C).
  9. Remove the roast from the marinade and discard the marinade.
  10. Place the roast in a roasting pan and roast for 2-2 1/2 hours, or until a meat thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the roast registers 145 degrees F (63 degrees C).
  11. Remove the roast from the oven and let it rest for 15 minutes before slicing
  12. Serve with the pan juices.
